A massive earthquake happens in the Pacific Northwest and people begin rebuilding—what will be rebuilt?

In a disaster, people move fast.

Money moves faster.

Buying and selling at the right moment.

Data.

Trends.

Speculation.

Imagined futures.

Investors place bets on an imagined return.

The future is uncertain, but we know a Cascadia subduction zone earthquake will happen.

We know hedge funds buy land in water-rich regions of the world.

Sometime in the future . . .

after a massive quake . . .

a warmer, drier planet makes this region even more desirable than it already is,

when lifelines and routines are disrupted for many months:

people without savings eventually default on their mortgages.

Farmers disrupted, vulnerable.

Can those properties . . .

homes and farms . . .

the land . . .

be purchased by out-of-state funds?

Will global finance be a predatory actor after a massive earthquake?

Now it is only speculation,

a novel about financial technology,

the protagonist is a college drop out and indie game savant—

when his team discovers the founders preparing for a crack up of civilization,

they rebel.
